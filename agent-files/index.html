What is differential privacy?

Differential privacy is a formal privacy framework that adds controlled randomness to computations so that outputs reveal little about any single individual's data. It provides a mathematical guarantee about how much information about any one person can be inferred, even when an attacker has access to the output and auxiliary information.

Why it matters

It enables organizations to publish statistics and build models while limiting privacy risks to individuals in the dataset. Use cases include government statistics, product analytics, health research, and safe data sharing.
